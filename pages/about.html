About Me

As a result I knew I wanted to be a video game programmer and make the kind of games that bring people and communities together.

My other hobbies include log rolling, swimming, stained glass, board games, history podcasts, and nature.

Work Experience

All of my jobs that I have worked while in college have been training me in communication and management. At the moment I am a swim coach for a local team called Indiana Swim Club (ISC). My time there has been spent coaching kid in the range all the way from elementary school to high school. Every day I make sure to choose my works carefully in order to explain how each participant can improve. This can be challenging in a group setting where children can disruptive and unresponsive but I do my best to keep the chaos under control. My one on one communication has been bolstered from my many years working for IU RecSports as a Swim Instructor. In this role I train people in private lessons where I construct a workout and guide the participant through. This position sees me teaching individuals ranging from children to adults how to swim. Lastly, I held the position of an Residential Assistant (RA) at IU during Covid19. While this role was stressful, it taught me a lot and gave me a chance to practice setting rules and expectations for everyone. Because of Covid, there were often policy changes from on high that I would have to implement in my day to day position. All of these jobs have honed my communication and management to a fine point and I feel confident in whatever role I take next.
